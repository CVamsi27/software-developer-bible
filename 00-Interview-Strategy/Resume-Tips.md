## The Senior Resume Mindset

A senior engineer's resume is fundamentally different from a mid-level one. Recruiters spend **6-8 seconds** on an initial scan. Your resume must immediately signal:

- **Impact over tasks** — Not what you did, but what changed because you did it
- **Leadership without titles** — Mentoring, architecture decisions, cross-team influence
- **Breadth + depth** — Full stack range with clear depth in key areas
- **Business awareness** — You understand why engineering decisions matter

> A senior resume answers: "What problems did you solve, and what was the measurable outcome?"

## Resume Formatting & Structure

### Recommended Layout

```text
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Name                                        │
│  Location | Email | Phone | LinkedIn | GitHub│
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Professional Summary (3-4 lines)            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Experience (reverse chronological)          │
│  ├── Role, Company, Dates                   │
│  │   ├── Bullet 1 (achievement)             │
│  │   ├── Bullet 2 (achievement)             │
│  │   └── Bullet 3 (achievement)             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Skills (grouped by category)                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Education                                   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Optional: Certifications, Side Projects     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘

```

### Formatting Rules

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Font | Inter, Calibri, or Garamond (10-11pt) |
| Margins | 0.5" - 0.75" |
| Bullet points | Start with strong action verbs |
| Length | 1 page (< 10 yrs), 2 pages (10+ yrs) |
| File format | PDF (preserve formatting) |
| File name | `Firstname-Lastname-Resume.pdf` |
| Sections | No more than 6-7 sections total |

### Section Order (Priority)

1. **Contact Info** — Keep it minimal

2. **Professional Summary** — Your elevator pitch

3. **Experience** — Core of the resume

4. **Technical Skills** — Keyword-rich

5. **Education** — Brief at senior level

6. **Certifications / Projects** — Optional differentiators

## Professional Summary

This is the most important 3-4 lines on your resume. It must answer: **Why should we interview you?**

### Bad Summary

```text
Experienced software engineer with 8+ years of experience in full stack
development. Proficient in JavaScript, React, Node.js. Passionate about
building great software. Team player with strong communication skills.

```

**Why it fails:** Generic, no quantifiable impact, could be anyone.

### Good Summary

```text
Senior Full Stack Engineer with 8 years building scalable fintech platforms
serving 2M+ users. Led migration from monolith to microservices, reducing
deployment time by 75%. Architected real-time payment processing system
handling $50M+ annual volume. Experienced in mentoring 12+ engineers and
driving engineering practices across 3 product teams.

```

**Why it works:** Specific domain, measurable outcomes, leadership evidence.

### Summary Template

```typescript
[Title] with [X] years building [domain/type of products] serving [scale].
[Key achievement #1 with metric]. [Key achievement #2 with metric].
[Leadership/culture contribution].

```

## Quantifying Achievements

This is the single most impactful thing you can do. Numbers create credibility.

### The Formula

```text
Action Verb + What You Built/Did + Measurable Outcome

```

### Transformation Examples

**Before:**

```text
• Worked on improving application performance
• Helped reduce bugs in the application
• Built new features for the dashboard
• Involved in code reviews and mentoring

```

**After:**

```text
• Optimized React rendering pipeline, reducing FCP from 3.2s to 0.8s (75% improvement)
  and improving Lighthouse score from 52 to 94 across 15 core pages
• Established code review process and QA automation, reducing production incidents
  from 12/month to 2/month (83% reduction) over 6 months
• Designed and shipped 8 new dashboard components using Storybook + TypeScript,
  reducing feature delivery time by 40% for 3 product teams
• Mentored 4 junior engineers through structured 1:1s and pair programming,
  with 3 receiving promotions within 18 months

```

### Metrics That Matter for Senior Engineers

| Category | Example Metrics |
|----------|----------------|
| **Performance** | Response time reduction (%), p99 latency, throughput |
| **Scale** | Users served, requests/second, data volume |
| **Reliability** | Uptime improved, incident reduction, MTTR |
| **Speed** | Deployment frequency, lead time, time-to-market |
| **Cost** | Infrastructure savings ($), resource optimization |
| **Quality** | Bug reduction (%), test coverage increase |
| **Leadership** | Engineers mentored, teams influenced, processes created |
| **Revenue** | Features that drove $X revenue, conversion improvements |

### Power Verbs for Senior Engineers

```text
Architected | Spearheaded | Championed | Drove | Pioneered
Optimized | Streamlined | Automated | Consolidated | Migrated
Mentored | Guided | Influenced | Established | Standardized
Delivered | Shipped | Launched | Scaled | Hardened
Reduced | Increased | Improved | Eliminated | Transformed

```

## Tech Stack Presentation

### Group by Category

Don't dump 30 technologies into one list. Organize them:

```text
Languages:      TypeScript, JavaScript (ES6+), Python, SQL, GraphQL
Frontend:       React 18, Next.js 14, Tailwind CSS, Redux Toolkit, Zustand
Backend:        Node.js, NestJS, Express, Fastify
Databases:      PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Redis, Elasticsearch
Cloud/DevOps:   AWS (EC2, ECS, Lambda, S3, RDS), Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform
Testing:        Jest, Cypress, Playwright, React Testing Library
Tools:          Git, GitHub Actions, Datadog, Sentry, Figma

```

### Skill Level Indicators (Optional)

If you want to show depth, use a subtle system:

```text
TypeScript ████████████████████ Expert
Python     ████████████████░░░░ Advanced
Go         ████████████░░░░░░░░ Intermediate
Rust       ████████░░░░░░░░░░░░ Learning

```

### What NOT to Do

- ❌ List technologies you used once in a workshop
- ❌ Include deprecated technologies (AngularJS, jQuery unless relevant)
- ❌ List "Microsoft Office" or basic tools
- ❌ Separate "languages" and "frameworks" into unrelated lists
- ❌ Forget to include the versions/ranges you work with

## Project Descriptions

### Structure for Each Role

```text
### Senior Full Stack Engineer — Company Name
#### Jan 2022 - Present | Remote

**Context:** One-line description of the product/domain/team size

**Key Achievements:**

- Achievement 1 (with metric)
- Achievement 2 (with metric)
- Achievement 3 (with metric)

**Tech Stack:** TypeScript, React, Next.js, Node.js, PostgreSQL, AWS, Docker

```

### Writing Achievement Bullets

**Framework:**

```text
[Action] [specific system/feature] using [technology], resulting in [metric/outcome]

```

**Examples:**

```text
• Architected event-driven order processing system using Node.js, Kafka, and
  PostgreSQL, handling 10K orders/day with 99.99% reliability

• Led frontend performance initiative across 4 React applications, implementing
  code splitting, lazy loading, and CDN optimization — reducing average page
  load from 4.1s to 1.2s for 500K monthly active users

• Designed and implemented multi-tenant RBAC system using NestJS and Casbin,
  enabling enterprise sales to 3 new Fortune 500 clients ($2.4M ARR)

• Established monorepo architecture using Turborepo + Nx, consolidating 6
  repositories into one, reducing CI/CD pipeline time from 25min to 8min

• Created automated deployment pipeline using GitHub Actions and ArgoCD,
  enabling 15+ daily deployments with zero-downtime rollbacks

```

### Project Section (For Side Projects / Open Source)

```text
### Notable Projects

**DevFlow** — Open source developer workflow tool
• Built CLI tool in Node.js/TypeScript that automates git workflows,
  reducing context switching by 60% for development teams
• 2.3K GitHub stars, 45+ contributors, used by engineering teams at 5 companies

**PaySplit** — Real-time payment splitting app

  using WebSockets and Stripe integration
```

## ATS Optimization

### What is ATS?

Applicant Tracking Systems (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, iCIMS) parse and filter resumes before a human sees them. If your resume isn't ATS-friendly, it may never reach a recruiter.

### ATS Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use standard section headings | Use creative headings ("Where I've Made My Mark") |
| Write full words | Use excessive abbreviations |
| Include job description keywords | Keyword stuff unnaturally |
| Use simple formatting | Use tables, columns, or text boxes |
| Save as PDF | Use .doc or .pages |
| Use standard bullet characters | Use custom icons or graphics |

### Keyword Strategy

1. **Read the job description** — Identify repeated terms and required skills

2. **Mirror their language** — If they say "microservices," don't write "distributed systems"

3. **Include both acronyms and full terms** — "CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment)"

4. **Weave keywords into achievements** — Don't just list them in a skills section

### Example: Keyword Integration

**Job Description Extract:**

```text
Looking for experience with React, Node.js, PostgreSQL, AWS, microservices,
CI/CD pipelines, and agile methodologies. Experience with performance
optimization and monitoring is a plus.

```

**Resume Integration:**

```text
• Architected microservices platform on AWS (ECS, Lambda, RDS PostgreSQL)
  using Node.js and TypeScript, serving 2M+ API requests/day

• Established CI/CD pipelines using GitHub Actions with automated testing
  and deployment, reducing release cycle from weekly to multiple daily

• Led React performance optimization initiative using Datadog RUM monitoring,
  improving Core Web Vitals across 15 pages (LCP: 4.2s → 1.1s)

```

## Common Mistakes

### Top 10 Senior Resume Mistakes

**1. Describing responsibilities instead of achievements**

```text
❌ "Responsible for developing frontend applications"
✅ "Built React component library adopted by 4 teams, reducing UI
    development time by 35%"

```

**2. Being too vague**

```text
❌ "Improved application performance"
✅ "Reduced API response time from 800ms to 120ms by implementing
    Redis caching layer and optimizing 12 PostgreSQL queries"

```

**3. Listing every technology ever touched**

```text
❌ "HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Angular, Vue, Node.js, Python, Java,
    C++, Ruby, PHP, Go, Rust, Swift, Kotlin..."
✅ Curated list of 12-15 most relevant technologies grouped by category

```

**4. Ignoring the job description**
Not tailoring your resume to match the specific role and company.

**5. Inconsistent formatting**
Mixed date formats, different bullet styles, varying section structures.

**6. No quantifiable results anywhere**
Zero metrics, numbers, or measurable outcomes in the entire resume.

**7. Missing keywords for ATS**
Using different terminology than the job posting.

**8. Outdated information**
Listing technologies from 10+ years ago that aren't relevant.

**9. Poor file naming**
`resume_v3_final_FINAL(2).pdf` → `Firstname-Lastname-Senior-Full-Stack.pdf`

**10. Typos and grammar errors**
At senior level, this signals carelessness. Have 2-3 people review it.

## One-Page vs Two-Page Debate

### One Page If:

- Less than 10 years of experience
- Applying to startups or smaller companies
- Career has been focused (same domain/stack)

### Two Pages If:

- 10+ years of relevant experience
- Significant open source, publications, or speaking
- Applying to FAANG or large enterprises
- Extensive leadership and mentoring experience

### The Golden Rule

> **Every line must earn its place.** If a line doesn't help you get the interview, cut it.

## Final Checklist

Before sending your resume, verify:

- [ ] Contact information is correct and professional email
- [ ] Summary is tailored to this specific role
- [ ] Every bullet starts with a strong action verb
- [ ] At least 60% of bullets have quantifiable metrics
- [ ] Tech stack matches job description keywords
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors (use Grammarly + human review)
- [ ] Formatting is consistent throughout
- [ ] PDF renders correctly on different devices
- [ ] File is named professionally
- [ ] No personal information (age, photo, marital status)
- [ ] LinkedIn profile matches resume content
- [ ] No "References available upon request" (obvious)
- [ ] No first-person pronouns ("I", "my", "me")
- [ ] Dates are consistent format (MM/YYYY or MMM YYYY)
- [ ] At least one achievement shows scale/impact

## Pro Tips

1. **Create a "master resume"** — Keep a comprehensive document with all achievements. Tailor from this master for each application.

2. **Track everything** — Keep a spreadsheet of applications, response rates, and which versions of your resume performed best.

3. **Update continuously** — Don't wait until you're job searching. Update your resume after every major project or achievement.

4. **Get senior engineer feedback** — Ask someone at your target company level to review. They know what hiring managers look for.

5. **Match the company's engineering blog** — If they write about specific challenges, highlight similar experience in your resume.

6. **The 30-second test** — Have someone scan your resume for 30 seconds and tell you what they remember. If they can't summarize your value, rewrite.

7. **Don't undersell or oversell** — Be honest about your level. Inflated claims get exposed in technical interviews.
